How to Clear Cache and Cookies on Any Web Browser

Posted • 2025 • by Ibrahim

Over time, your browser stores temporary files (cache) and small bits of data (cookies) to make websites load faster and remember your logins. That’s handy — until it causes slowdowns, privacy concerns, or weird glitches. Clearing cache and cookies can often fix pages that won’t load or display correctly. Here’s how to do it on the most common browsers.

1. Google Chrome (Desktop)

  • Click the three dots at the top-right.
  • Go to More Tools → Clear browsing data…
  • Choose a time range (last hour, last 24 hours, all time).
  • Tick Cookies and other site data and Cached images and files.
  • Click Clear data.

2. Google Chrome (Android / iOS)

  • Tap the three dots (Android) or More (iOS).
  • Tap History → Clear browsing data…
  • Select time range and tick Cookies and Cached images and files.
  • Tap Clear data.

3. Microsoft Edge (Desktop)

  • Click the three dots in the upper right.
  • Choose Settings → Privacy, search, and services.
  • Under Clear browsing data, click Choose what to clear.
  • Tick Cookies and other site data and Cached images and files.
  • Click Clear now.

4. Mozilla Firefox (Desktop)

  • Click the hamburger menu (three lines).
  • Choose Settings → Privacy & Security.
  • Scroll to Cookies and Site Data → click Clear Data…
  • Tick Cookies and Site Data and Cached Web Content.
  • Click Clear.

5. Mozilla Firefox (Android / iOS)

  • Tap the three dots → Settings.
  • Tap Delete browsing data.
  • Select Cookies and Cached images and files.
  • Tap Delete browsing data.

6. Safari (macOS)

Clear Cache: Safari → Preferences → Advanced → tick Show Develop menu. Then Develop → Empty Caches.

Clear Cookies: Safari → Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data → Remove All.

7. Safari (iPhone / iPad)

  • Open Settings → Safari.
  • Scroll to Clear History and Website Data and tap it.
  • Confirm.

8. Opera (Desktop)

  • Click the O menu top left.
  • Go to History → Clear browsing data.
  • Select time range, tick Cookies and Cached images/files, then Clear data.

9. Brave Browser (Desktop)

  • Click the three lines (or the Brave logo menu).
  • Go to Settings → Privacy and security → Clear browsing data.
  • Choose time range, tick Cookies and Cached images and files, then Clear data.

Quick Keyboard Shortcut

Most browsers open the “Clear browsing data” box with Ctrl + Shift + Delete (Windows) or Command + Shift + Delete (Mac).

Tips

  • Clearing cookies signs you out of websites; save your passwords first.
  • Clearing cache won’t delete bookmarks or downloads.
  • Do this regularly if you’re troubleshooting site issues or want more privacy.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re using Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Opera, or Brave, clearing cache and cookies only takes a few seconds and can solve a surprising number of browsing problems. Plus, it’s a good privacy habit.
