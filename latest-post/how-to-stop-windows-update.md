Windows Updates are meant to improve your PC, but sometimes they pop up at the worst moments, eat bandwidth, or break something that was working. Below are three proven ways to stop them — from the quick temporary pause to a deeper, more permanent block.

## 1. Temporarily Pause Updates (Fastest and Built-In)

- Press **Windows Key + I** to open Settings.  
- Click **Windows Update**.  
- Click **Pause updates** and choose how long.  

✅ Done — Windows Update won’t run until your chosen date expires.

---

## 2. Disable the Windows Update Service (Semi-Permanent)

- Press **Windows Key + R**, type `services.msc`, hit Enter.  
- Find **Windows Update**.  
- Double-click it. In **Startup type**, select **Disabled**.  
- Click **Stop** then **Apply** and **OK**.  

---

## 3. Group Policy or Registry (More Permanent)

- Press **Windows Key + R**, type `gpedit.msc` or `regedit` (depending on edition).  
- Follow the paths described to disable Automatic Updates.  

---

## Final Thoughts

No method is truly 100% permanent but these three options give you control — quick pause, semi-permanent service disable, or deeper Group Policy/Registry tweak.
